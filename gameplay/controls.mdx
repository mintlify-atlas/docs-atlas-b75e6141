## Control Overview

Forsaken Casino uses a **gaze-based interaction system** combined with standard first-person controls. The game is designed for both VR headsets and desktop browsers, using A-Frame's built-in control systems.

## Gaze-Based Cursor System

The primary interaction method uses a **raycaster cursor** that detects when you look at interactive objects.

### Cursor Configuration

```html
<a-cursor id="cursor" 
          raycaster="objects: .clickable" 
          material="color: #ff0000" 
          scale="0.5 0.5 0.5">
</a-cursor>
```

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visual Appearance">
    The cursor appears as a **red reticle** in the center of your vision.
    
    - **Color**: Bright red (`#ff0000`) for high visibility against dark environment
    - **Scale**: 0.5× default size for precision targeting
    - **Shape**: Ring/torus (A-Frame default cursor geometry)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Raycaster Target">
    The cursor only interacts with elements that have the `clickable` class:
    
    ```javascript
    card.setAttribute('class', 'clickable');
    ```
    
    This prevents accidental interactions with the environment (table, walls, floor).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Parent Entity">
    The cursor is a child of the camera entity, so it always stays centered in your field of view:
    
    ```html
    <a-entity id="camera" camera look-controls wasd-controls="acceleration: 10">
      <a-cursor id="cursor" ... ></a-cursor>
    </a-entity>
    ```
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Hover Interaction

Cards use the `mouseenter` event for gaze interaction:

```javascript
card.addEventListener('mouseenter', function() {
    if (lockBoard || this.dataset.flipped === "true" || timeLeft <= 0) return;
    
    startTimer();
    new Audio(document.querySelector('#click-sound').src).play();
    flip(this);
});
```

<Info>
  In A-Frame, `mouseenter` is triggered when the raycaster cursor intersects with an object. No clicking required—just **look at a card to flip it**.
</Info>

<Warning>
  This is a **hover-based** system, not click-based. Be careful where you look! The moment your cursor touches a valid card, it will flip automatically.
</Warning>

## Look Controls

The camera uses A-Frame's `look-controls` component for head/mouse rotation.

### Configuration

```html
<a-entity id="camera" camera look-controls wasd-controls="acceleration: 10" rotation="-25 0 0">
```

### Desktop Controls

<Accordion title="Mouse Look">
  **Click and drag** to rotate the camera:
  - Horizontal mouse movement → Yaw (left/right rotation)
  - Vertical mouse movement → Pitch (up/down rotation)
  
  You can also use the **pointer lock** feature by clicking anywhere in the scene to capture the mouse.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Touch Look (Mobile/Tablet)">
  **Touch and drag** on the screen to rotate the camera.
</Accordion>

### VR Controls

<Accordion title="Head Tracking">
  When using a VR headset (Oculus, Vive, etc.), the camera automatically follows your head orientation using the device's IMU sensors.
  
  <Tip>
    The gaze cursor moves with your head, allowing you to flip cards by simply looking at them—perfect for VR immersion.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

### Initial Camera Angle

The camera starts tilted downward for optimal table viewing:

```html
rotation="-25 0 0"
```

- **Pitch**: -25° (looking down at the table)
- **Yaw**: 0° (facing forward)
- **Roll**: 0° (no tilt)

<Info>
  This initial angle ensures the card table is immediately visible without needing to adjust your view.
</Info>

## WASD Movement Controls

The camera rig includes keyboard-based movement for navigation.

### Configuration

```html
<a-entity id="rig" position="0 1.6 1.2">
  <a-entity id="camera" camera look-controls wasd-controls="acceleration: 10">
```

### Movement Keys

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="W" icon="arrow-up">
    Move **forward** in the direction you're facing
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="S" icon="arrow-down">
    Move **backward** opposite to your facing direction
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="A" icon="arrow-left">
    **Strafe left** perpendicular to facing direction
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="D" icon="arrow-right">
    **Strafe right** perpendicular to facing direction
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Movement Parameters

```javascript
wasd-controls="acceleration: 10"
```

<Info>
  **Acceleration**: Set to 10 units/s² for responsive movement. Higher than default for quicker repositioning around the table.
</Info>

Default WASD controls also include:
- **Max speed**: ~4 units/second (A-Frame default)
- **Easing**: Gradual acceleration and deceleration
- **Collision**: Disabled by default (can move through objects)

### Arrow Keys Alternative

<Tip>
  A-Frame's `wasd-controls` also supports **arrow keys** as an alternative to WASD for movement.
</Tip>

## Camera Rig Positioning

The camera is mounted on a rig entity that controls the player's base position:

```html
<a-entity id="rig" position="0 1.6 1.2">
```

### Starting Position

- **X**: `0` (centered horizontally)
- **Y**: `1.6` (eye level, standard VR height)
- **Z**: `1.2` (positioned back from table)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Height (Y: 1.6)">
    Places the camera at approximately 1.6 meters (5'3"), simulating standing eye level. This is the standard VR height for seated or standing experiences.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Distance (Z: 1.2)">
    Positions the player 1.2 meters back from the table center (Z=0), providing an optimal viewing distance for the 4×2 card grid.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Centering (X: 0)">
    Aligns the player directly in front of the table's center for symmetrical card viewing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Table Position Reference

The game board is positioned relative to the rig:

```html
<a-entity id="board" position="-0.3 0.85 0.2" rotation="-90 0 0" scale="0.5 0.5 0.5"></a-entity>
```

With the rig at (0, 1.6, 1.2) and the board at (-0.3, 0.85, 0.2):
- **Viewing angle**: Looking down from ~0.75 meters above the table
- **Horizontal offset**: Slightly left (-0.3) for natural positioning
- **Distance**: About 1 meter forward from player position

## HUD Elements

Several UI elements are attached to the camera for always-visible feedback.

### Timer Display

```html
<a-text id="timerText" 
        value="VIDA: 40s" 
        color="red" 
        position="0 0.5 -1" 
        scale="0.4 0.4 0.4" 
        align="center">
</a-text>
```

<Info>
  **Position**: 0.5 units **above** camera center, 1 unit **forward**  
  **Result**: Appears in the upper field of view, always visible without obstructing card view
</Info>

### Death Overlay

```html
<a-plane id="deathOverlay" 
         position="0 0 -0.05" 
         width="2" height="2" 
         color="#300" 
         material="opacity: 0; transparent: true">
</a-plane>
```

<Accordion title="Overlay Functionality">
  Positioned directly in front of the camera (0.05 units forward):
  - **Initial state**: Fully transparent (opacity: 0)
  - **On death**: Fades to opaque dark red over 1 second
  - **Size**: 2×2 units fills entire view
  - **Color**: Dark crimson (`#300`) for ominous effect
  
  ```javascript
  deathOverlay.setAttribute('animation', 
    'property: material.opacity; to: 1; dur: 1000');
  ```
</Accordion>

### Status Text

Large floating text for win/lose messages:

```html
<a-text id="statusText" 
        value="" 
        color="red" 
        position="0 2 -3" 
        scale="2 2 2" 
        align="center">
</a-text>
```

<Info>
  **Position**: 2 units **above** ground, 3 units **forward** (world-space, not camera-relative)  
  **Result**: Appears as large text floating above the table when win/lose conditions trigger
</Info>

## VR Headset Support

The game automatically adapts to VR headsets through A-Frame's WebXR implementation.

### Entering VR Mode

<Steps>
  <Step title="VR Button">
    A-Frame automatically adds a "Enter VR" button in the bottom-right corner when a VR device is detected.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Device Detection">
    Supports WebXR-compatible devices:
    - Meta Quest 1/2/3/Pro
    - HTC Vive
    - Valve Index
    - Windows Mixed Reality
    - Any WebXR-compatible browser
  </Step>

  <Step title="Gaze Control">
    In VR mode, the red cursor follows your head orientation, allowing you to flip cards by looking at them naturally.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Movement in VR

<Warning>
  WASD controls may not work in VR mode depending on the device. Most VR experiences use room-scale movement or controller-based locomotion, but this game is designed for **stationary gameplay** at a table.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  For the best VR experience, position yourself at a comfortable standing or seated distance from the table before entering VR mode.
</Tip>

## Control Limitations

<Accordion title="No Click Requirement">
  Unlike traditional games, you **cannot** click to interact. The gaze cursor automatically triggers card flips on hover (`mouseenter` event). Be mindful of cursor placement.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="No Jump or Crouch">
  The WASD controls only allow horizontal movement (X/Z plane). Vertical position (Y) is fixed at 1.6 meters.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="No Collision Detection">
  You can move through the table and other objects. The game assumes you'll stay in the intended play area.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="No Controller Input">
  VR controllers are not configured for input. All interaction is gaze-based, making it universally compatible with any VR headset (including those without hand controllers).
</Accordion>
